# Interlude 064 – Schedule

> It was roughly 20:30 when Solana stopped by and asked whether Vic wanted to spend the evening together.
>
> At that moment, Vic had just completed his evening ritual—and he didn't exactly make plans for further into the night. Yet, Solana was now standing in front of him, with Clementine shortly behind, having just configured the lab portal to his address.
>
> To his surprise, Vic wasn't taken aback by her invite. If they were strangers, sure, the visit would have been completely disruptive. Knowing Solana, however, he wholly welcomed it. To him, this was a remarkably normal opportunity. Refreshingly so, even.

---

> As he welcomes her, Vic tells Solana, "I'm very grateful for your presence, and I do so enjoy our time together."
>
> He then adds, his tone somewhat grandiose, "You _must_ admit, though, should this become a regular occurrence, we should work out a schedule?"
>
> He could see the switch in Solana's expression. The change in tone was indicative of one of his games, after all, and she knew she would need to work it out.
>
> With the corner of her lips upturned, Solana throws Vic a blithe expression.
> 
> "Go on?" she says.
>
> "Very well," he replies. "I propose we plan these visits on a weekly basis. This time window in which you arrived today was perfect, as were the previous times you arrived."
>
> He now focuses on Solana intently. "As for the day of the week... well, there's only one option that makes any sense, isn't there?"

---

> He figured Solana knew he had wordplay in mind. As expected, upon realizing what he was getting at, Solana breaks into a soft laugh.
>
> "Knowing your fondness for ice cream, you must have been holding onto that pun for a while," she answers.
>
> Actually, he only came across the pun recently—but this wasn't the time to share that. After all, Solana's solution was incomplete.
> 
> "What else?" Vic inquires.
>
> Solana rolls her eyes and sighs warmly. "...And knowing your love of double-layered puns, you had to choose that day of the week because of me."
>
> There we are.
>
> Vic nods and lightly presses her nose against her cheek. "You are a brilliant spot of sun in my life, after all," he says.
>
> Before he even finished the sentence, Solana had begun to shrink into her clothes.

---

> It was only five minutes in, and Vic could already tell this evening would be an enjoyable one.
